# Thoughts on Teaching Computational Biology

*February 10, 2026*

Teaching students to code and think computationally is a privilege. Over the past year, I've learned that the best teaching moments happen when students struggle productively with hard problems, not when I lecture perfectly.

## Learning Through Struggle

Students often expect me to have all the answers. But the reality is, good programming requires debugging, iterating, and learning from mistakes.

## Creating a Safe Space to Fail

The most important thing I can do as an educator is create an environment where students feel comfortable failing. Because failure is where learning happens.

## What I've Learned

- Live-code messy examples, not polished slides
- Answer questions with questions
- Celebrate debugging sessions, not just final solutions
- Let students struggle (but not too much)

The goal isn't to produce perfect programmers. It's to produce curious scientists who can use computation as a tool for discovery.
