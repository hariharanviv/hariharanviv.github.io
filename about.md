# Vivek Hariharan

**Ph.D. student, Johns Hopkins University**

Hi — I'm Vivek Hariharan. I'm a current Ph.D. student at Johns Hopkins University, studying HIV-1 persistence in the [Siliciano lab](https://silicianolab.jhu.edu/). My Ph.D. focuses on understanding the immune response against HIV-1 and creating high-throughput assays to measure an individual's own immune response against the viruses which infect them. I also had a side project, studying non-suppressible viremia, which is when a person living with HIV-1 has a viral load that cannot be suppressed by antiretroviral therapy. As they so often do, [this side project](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41564-025-02135-z) was published before my main thesis project. 

I'm looking to wrap up my Ph.D. in the next few months and start as a postdoctoral fellow in [David Liu's lab](https://www.liugroup.us/). 

## What is this blog?

As I went through my Ph.D., I realized that I have an interest in sharing how I view the world, mostly from a data-driven viewpoint. Science (which I am loosely defining as collecting data and analyzing it) can extend far beyond the bench. I will try to illustrate that here. 

I hope that you see the world a bit differently after reading some of these posts and it makes you look at the world with a new perspective. I am inspired by other blogs from professors [Kenny Matreyek](https://visualizedlife.com/) and [Jean Fan](https://jef.works/blog/). Both of these blogs attempt to analyze the world through a data-driven lens, and I hope to do the same here.

### A short note on AI

In today's age, I find that AI is dominating writing (as it should). For scientific writing, [one study](https://www.science.org/content/article/far-more-authors-use-ai-write-science-papers-admit-it-publisher-reports) found that 36% of abstracts submitted to the American Association for Cancer Research (AACR) contained AI-generated text. [Another study from the Ottawa Hospital Research Institute in Canada](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39550312/) found that 45% of respondents said they used AI chatbots to aid medical research. While this may be a good step for a variety of reasons (perhaps a topic for a future blog post), it results in writing which lacks voice. 

Since this blog is aimed at sharing my raw thoughts, **I will not utilize AI for any writing** (except for spell check). Of course AI will be used to aid in research, figure generation, analysis of data, and creation of this website.
